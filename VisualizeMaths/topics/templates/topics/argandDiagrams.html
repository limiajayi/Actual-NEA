Argand Diagrams

Modulus and arguments

For a complex number \( z = a + bi \):

$$ │z│ = \sqrt{ a^2 + b^2 } $$ $$ arg(z) = \theta = tan^-1 (\frac{b}{a}) $$

Complex numbers have a complex conjugate \( z^* = a - bi \) Where \( z = re^i\theta \) and \( z^* = re^-i\theta \)

Multiplication and Division of complex numbers

For a complex numbers in their modulus-arguments form of \( z_1 = r(cos \theta_1 + isin \theta_1 ) \) and \( z_2 = r(cos \theta_2 + isin \theta_2 ) \)

$$ z_1z_2 = r_1r_2(cos(\theta_1 + \theta_2) + isin(\theta_1 + \theta_2)) $$ $$ \frac{z_1}{z_2} = \frac{r_1}{r_2} (cos(\theta_1 - \theta_2) + isin(\theta_1 - \theta_2)) $$

Loci in argand diagrams

\( z = a + bi \)

$$ │ z - a │ = k $$

Circle of radius k centred on (Re(a), Im(a))

$$ │ z - a │ = │ z - b │ $$

Perpendicular bisector of the line from a to b

$$ arg(z - a) = \alpha $$

A half-line starting from a making an angle \( \alpha \) with the real axis.

De Moivre's Theorem

$$ 2cos \theta = e^{i\theta} + e^{-i\theta} $$ $$ 2isin \theta = e^{i\theta} - e^{-i\theta} $$ $$ (z + \frac{1}{z} ) = 2cos \theta $$ $$ (z^n + \frac{1}{z^n} ) = 2cos n\theta $$ $$ (z - \frac{1}{z} ) = 2isin \theta $$ $$ (z^n - \frac{1}{z^n} ) = 2isin n\theta $$
