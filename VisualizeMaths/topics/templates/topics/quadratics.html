Solving Quadratics

For an equation \( y = f(x) \) where $$ f(x) = ax^2 + bx + c $$ The roots of the equation, where \( y = 0 \) can be found by using the quadratic formula $$ \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a} $$

Number of Roots

  • If \( b^2 - 4ac \gt 0 \) there are two real roots and the curve crosses the x-axis twice
  • If \( b^2 - 4ac = 0 \) there is only one real root and the curve touches the x-axis once
  • If \( b^2 - 4ac \lt 0 \) there are no real roots and the curve does not intersect the x-axis

Sketching Quadratics

For an equation \( y = f(x) \) where $$ f(x) = ax^2 + bx + c $$ If the coefficent of \( x^2 \) is positive, the graph is convex and forms a U shape. If the coefficent of \( x^2 \) is negative, the graph is concave and forms an n shape.
